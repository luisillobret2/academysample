# 9. Metrics & KPIs

## Design Philosophy

Metrics follow the **AARRR framework** adapted for partner enablement: **Acquisition** (are partners joining?), **Activation** (are they learning?), **Retention** (do they keep learning?), **Revenue** (does learning drive deals?), **Referral** (do they bring more partners?). Every metric must connect to a business outcome, not just a learning activity.

**WHY AARRR**: Most LMS dashboards track vanity metrics (course views, video plays) that don't correlate with partner performance. The AARRR framework ensures every metric ladder up to revenue impact. If a metric can't answer "so what?" for a channel VP, it shouldn't be on the dashboard.

---

## Primary KPIs (Executive Dashboard)

### 1. Monthly Active Learners (MAL)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | Unique users who complete at least 1 learning activity (module, lab, quiz, or roleplay) in a calendar month |
| **Target** | 60% of registered partner users are MAL within 6 months of launch |
| **Measurement** | User activity events aggregated monthly |
| **Benchmark** | Industry average for partner learning platforms: 25-35% MAL |
| **WHY** | MAL is the leading indicator of platform health. Low MAL means content isn't engaging or partners don't see value. High MAL correlates with higher certification rates and deal activity. |

### 2. Course Completion Rate

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | % of started learning paths that are completed within 90 days |
| **Target** | 65% completion rate (industry average: 30-40%) |
| **Measurement** | (Completed paths / Started paths) per 90-day cohort |
| **Segmentation** | By role, partner type, content type, and level |
| **WHY** | Completion rate measures content quality and journey design. Low completion on specific modules indicates content problems. Overall completion rate predicts certification pipeline. |

### 3. Certification Rate

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | % of eligible partners who earn at least one certification within 6 months of registration |
| **Target** | 40% certification rate |
| **Measurement** | (Certified users / Eligible users) by 6-month cohort |
| **Segmentation** | By certification level, partner type, partner tier |
| **WHY** | Certification is the conversion event that transitions a "learner" into a "competent partner." Certified partners generate 3-5x more pipeline (industry benchmark). Low certification rates indicate exam difficulty, content gaps, or insufficient motivation. |

### 4. Partner Activation Rate

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | % of new partners who complete Level 1 Foundation Track within 30 days of registration |
| **Target** | 80% activation within 30 days |
| **Measurement** | (Activated partners / New registrations) by 30-day cohort |
| **WHY** | Activation is the earliest predictor of partner success. Partners who don't complete foundations within 30 days are 5x more likely to be inactive at 90 days. This metric drives investment in onboarding UX and welcome sequences. |

### 5. Revenue per Certified Partner

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | Average annual Mend.io revenue generated by partners with at least one certification vs. uncertified partners |
| **Target** | Certified partners generate 3x more revenue than uncertified |
| **Measurement** | Allbound deal data + certification status → revenue attribution |
| **Segmentation** | By certification level, partner tier, product sold |
| **WHY** | This is the ultimate ROI metric for the learning platform. If certified partners don't generate more revenue, the program isn't working. This metric justifies continued investment in Mend Learn. |

### 6. Partner Engagement Score

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | Composite score (0-100) combining learning activity, certification status, deal activity, and community participation |
| **Formula** | (0.3 x Learning Score) + (0.25 x Certification Score) + (0.25 x Deal Activity Score) + (0.2 x Community Score) |
| **Target** | Average partner engagement score > 60 within 12 months |
| **Measurement** | Calculated weekly, trended monthly |
| **WHY** | Single metrics can be misleading (high learning, zero deals = disengaged). A composite score provides a holistic view of partner health. It's the number a channel VP looks at to assess program effectiveness. |

## Secondary KPIs (Operational Dashboard)

### 7. Sales Velocity (Time-to-First-Deal)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | Median number of days from partner registration to first deal registration in Allbound |
| **Target** | < 60 days for certified partners (industry average: 90-120 days) |
| **Measurement** | Registration date → first deal registration date |
| **Segmentation** | Certified vs. uncertified, by partner type |
| **WHY** | Fast time-to-first-deal indicates effective enablement. This metric validates that the learning content actually prepares partners to sell. Slow TFD suggests the sales track needs improvement or deal registration is too complex. |

### 8. Pipeline Influence

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | Total pipeline $ associated with partners who have at least one certified team member |
| **Target** | 70% of partner pipeline comes from orgs with certified members |
| **Measurement** | Allbound pipeline data cross-referenced with certification status |
| **Segmentation** | By certification level, product, partner tier |
| **WHY** | Pipeline influence connects learning investment to future revenue. It's a leading indicator (pipeline) rather than lagging indicator (closed revenue), so it's actionable sooner. |

### 9. Content Engagement Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Average session duration | Time spent per learning session | > 20 minutes |
| Modules per session | Average modules started per visit | > 1.5 |
| Lab start-to-completion rate | % of started labs that are completed | > 75% |
| Knowledge check pass rate | % of first-attempt passes on quizzes | 70-80% (below 70% = too hard, above 90% = too easy) |
| Video completion rate | % of video watched (not abandoned) | > 80% |
| AI roleplay sessions/user/month | Average roleplay sessions per user | > 2 |
| Search-to-content click rate | % of searches that lead to content engagement | > 60% |
| Content NPS | "Would you recommend this module?" | > 40 NPS |

### 10. Certification Pipeline Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Study plan starts | Users who begin a certification study plan | 50% of eligible users |
| Practice exam attempts | Average attempts before real exam | 2-3 attempts |
| Exam pass rate | First-attempt pass rate | 65-75% |
| Certification renewal rate | % of expired certs that are renewed | > 80% |
| Average time to certification | Days from first module to cert earned | < 60 days (Associate), < 120 days (Professional) |

### 11. Gamification Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Daily active learners (DAL) | Unique users with activity per day | > 15% of registered users |
| Average streak length | Consecutive days of learning activity | > 5 days |
| Badge collection rate | Average badges per user | > 5 within 6 months |
| Leaderboard engagement | % of users who view leaderboards monthly | > 40% |
| Challenge participation | % of eligible users who attempt quarterly challenges | > 30% |
| Referral rate | % of users who invite a colleague | > 10% |

## Reporting Framework

### Dashboard Tiers

| Dashboard | Audience | Refresh | Key Metrics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Summary | VP Channel, CRO | Weekly | MAL, Certification Rate, Revenue/Certified Partner, Pipeline Influence |
| Program Health | Partner Enablement Team | Daily | Activation Rate, Completion Rate, Content Engagement, Cert Pipeline |
| Content Performance | Content Team, LXD | Daily | Module-level completion, NPS, quiz pass rates, lab completion |
| Partner Scorecard | Partner Managers | Real-time | Individual partner org metrics, team progress, cert status |
| Self-Service | Individual Partners | Real-time | Personal progress, XP, badges, certification readiness |

```
Mend Learn LMS ──► Learning activity data
                   ├── Module completions
                   ├── Lab activity
                   ├── Quiz scores
                   ├── Certification status
                   ├── XP / badges
                   └── AI feature usage

Allbound PRM ────► Partner business data
                   ├── Deal registrations
                   ├── Pipeline value
                   ├── Revenue (closed)
                   ├── Partner tier
                   └── MDF usage

CRM (Salesforce) ► Revenue attribution
                   ├── Partner-sourced deals
                   ├── Partner-influenced deals

              ┌─────────────────────┐
```

## Attribution Model

### How to Credit Learning to Revenue

| Attribution Type | Definition | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Direct Attribution | Deal closed by a certified rep | 100% credit to learning |
| Influenced Attribution | Deal where at least one team member is certified | 50% credit |
| Correlated Attribution | Deal from a partner org with > 50% certification rate | 25% credit |
| Time-Decay Attribution | Credit decays based on recency of certification (full credit if certified < 6 months, 50% if 6-12 months) | Variable |

### Revenue Attribution Report

```
QUARTERLY LEARNING ROI REPORT

Total partner revenue: $X,000,000
  Revenue from certified partners: $Y,000,000 (Z%)
  Revenue from uncertified partners: $W,000,000

Average deal size:
  Certified partner deals: $A
  Uncertified partner deals: $B
  Lift: +C%

Sales cycle:
  Certified partner average: D days
  Uncertified partner average: E days
  Reduction: -F days

Win rate:
  Certified partner: G%
  Uncertified partner: H%
  Lift: +I%

Investment:
  Learning platform cost: $J/year
  Content creation cost: $K/year
  Total investment: $L/year

ROI: (Revenue lift attributable to learning) / (Total investment) = X:1
```

**WHY attribution**: Without revenue attribution, Mend Learn is a cost center. With attribution, it's a revenue driver. This report is the tool that secures ongoing executive investment in the platform. Cisco's Partner Enablement team credits their attribution model with securing a 3x budget increase over 3 years.

## Measurement Cadence

| Frequency | Review | Actions |
|---|---|---|
| Daily | Content team reviews engagement anomalies | Fix broken content, respond to low scores |
| Weekly | Program team reviews activation and completion | Adjust nudge sequences, flag at-risk partners |
| Monthly | Leadership reviews MAL, certification rate, pipeline | Strategic content decisions, resource allocation |
| Quarterly | Executive reviews revenue attribution and ROI | Program investment decisions, tier adjustments |
| Annually | Full program assessment and benchmarking | Roadmap planning, competitive analysis |
