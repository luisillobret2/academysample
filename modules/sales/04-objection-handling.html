Objection Handling & Closing

🕒 40 min 📚 4 sections ⭐ 150 XP Level 2 - Intermediate

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Apply a repeatable framework for any objection
  • Respond to the most common price, incumbent, and timing objections
  • Use trial and POC success criteria to advance the deal
  • Ask for the close and drive deal registration

The LAER Framework

Handle objections with a calm, consistent motion instead of reacting defensively.

  1. Listen — let them finish; do not interrupt.
  2. Acknowledge — “That’s a fair concern.”
  3. Explore — “Help me understand what’s behind that.”
  4. Respond — address the real issue, then confirm you resolved it.

Common Objections

ObjectionResponse angle
“You’re more expensive.”Reframe on cost of noise and time-to-fix; quantify hours saved by auto-remediation.
“We already have Snyk.”Offer a side-by-side on the same repo; let reachability results speak.
“Now isn’t the right time.”Tie to their compelling event (audit, incident, CRA deadline).
“Developers won’t adopt it.”Show IDE and PR-native workflow; run a short developer pilot.

⚠️ Avoid Discounting Too Early

A price objection is usually a value gap. Re-establish value before you ever touch the number.

Closing & Deal Registration

Advance with a defined POC and clear success criteria, then ask for the business.

  • Agree measurable POC exit criteria up front (e.g., noise reduction %, MTTR).
  • Summarize value in the buyer’s own metrics before proposing.
  • Use a trial close: “If we hit these criteria, is there any reason we couldn’t move forward?”
  • Register the deal in Allbound to lock your margin and unlock incentives.

💰 Registered Deals Win

Certified partners who register early receive priority support, better margins, and lead routing. Register at qualification, not at close.
