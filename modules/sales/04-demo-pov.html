Delivering Impactful Demos

The demo is often the most critical moment in the sales cycle. A great demo isn't about showing every feature — it's about connecting Mend's capabilities to the prospect's specific pain points.

Demo Preparation Checklist

  • Discovery Review — What are the prospect's top 3 pain points? Which competitors are they evaluating?
  • Environment Setup — Pre-configure a demo environment with a project similar to the prospect's tech stack
  • Story Arc — Structure the demo as a narrative: problem → discovery → solution → impact
  • Tailored Scenarios — Show features that directly address discovered pain points; skip features that aren't relevant
  • Live Scanning — If possible, scan one of the prospect's actual repositories live

Demo Flow: The 20-Minute Story

The ideal demo follows this structure:

  1. Minutes 1-3: Set the Stage — Confirm pain points from discovery. "Last time we spoke, you mentioned alert fatigue was your biggest challenge..."
  2. Minutes 4-8: First Scan — Run a live scan showing how quickly Mend identifies vulnerabilities. Highlight reachability analysis filtering.
  3. Minutes 9-13: Developer Experience — Show PR integration, IDE plugin, and how developers see and fix vulnerabilities without leaving their workflow.
  4. Minutes 14-17: Policy & Reporting — Demonstrate enterprise controls: custom policies, dashboards, compliance reports.
  5. Minutes 18-20: Close — Summarize value, propose next steps (POV, technical deep-dive, or commercial discussion).

Proof of Value (POV) Framework

A successful POV turns evaluation into commitment. Structure it with clear success criteria:

POV Structure

  • Duration — 14 days (resist pressure to extend; shorter POVs close faster)
  • Scope — 3-5 representative repositories covering the prospect's key tech stacks
  • Success Criteria — Define 3-5 measurable outcomes upfront (e.g., "identify 50% more reachable vulnerabilities than current tool")
  • Stakeholders — Security lead, engineering manager, and at least 2 developers should participate
  • Checkpoints — Day 1 kickoff, Day 7 mid-point review, Day 14 results presentation

💡 Pro Tip

Always agree on success criteria BEFORE the POV starts. This prevents "moving goalposts" and gives you a clear path to a purchase decision when criteria are met.

Post-Demo Follow-Up

The demo isn't over when the call ends. Effective follow-up determines whether momentum continues:

  • Within 1 hour — Send a summary email with key findings, demo recording link, and proposed next steps
  • Within 24 hours — Share a customized ROI analysis based on the demo findings
  • Within 1 week — Follow up with additional resources relevant to the prospect's specific use cases
  • Ongoing — Nurture champions with relevant content (case studies, webinars, new feature announcements)
