Partner Program & Tier Benefits

🕒 30 min 📚 4 sections ⭐ 150 XP Sales

🎯 Learning Objectives

In this module you will cover:

  • The four partner tiers — Silver, Gold, Platinum, and Diamond — and what distinguishes them
  • The certification, revenue, and business-plan requirements to reach and hold each tier
  • How deal registration discounts, lead routing, and MDF work across tiers
  • How to plan a deliberate progression from one tier to the next

The Partner Tier Structure

The Mend.io partner program is organized into four ascending tiers that reward increasing commitment, competency, and revenue contribution. Each step up delivers richer margins, more program support, and greater go-to-market investment from Mend. The tiers exist to create a clear, meritocratic path: partners who invest in certification and drive revenue are rewarded with the economics and support that make the next level of investment worthwhile.

The lowest tier, Silver, is the entry point for partners who have signed the program agreement and completed baseline enablement. Gold recognizes partners with a proven track record of registered deals and certified staff. Platinum is reserved for strategic partners with significant annual revenue, deep technical bench strength, and a joint business plan. Diamond sits at the top for the small number of elite partners who function as true extensions of Mend's own go-to-market, with the highest revenue commitments and executive-level alignment.

As a seller working with or through partners, knowing a partner's tier tells you immediately what discounting, support, and co-selling resources are available on a given opportunity. It also tells you where the partner is trying to go — and that progression goal is often a powerful shared incentive to close and expand deals together.

Certification and Annual Requirements

Every tier carries requirements that a partner must meet to attain the tier and maintain it year over year. These requirements fall into three buckets: competency (certified sales and technical staff), revenue (annual booked or influenced revenue), and commitment (a business plan and joint activities). Requirements scale with the tier — Diamond partners must maintain far more certified engineers and hit far higher revenue thresholds than Silver partners.

Certifications are the most controllable requirement and often the fastest lever for advancement. Sales certifications validate that a partner's account executives can position Mend and qualify opportunities, while technical certifications validate that solution engineers can scope, demo, and support deployments. Higher tiers require a minimum number of each, ensuring the partner can both sell and deliver without leaning entirely on Mend resources.

Requirements are re-evaluated annually. A partner that lets certifications lapse or misses its revenue threshold can be down-leveled at the annual review, so maintaining a tier is an ongoing commitment rather than a one-time achievement. The best partners treat certification as a continuous program, backfilling as staff turn over and pre-certifying ahead of promotions.

💡 Advancement Tip

Certifications are usually the quickest path to the next tier because they are entirely within the partner's control — unlike revenue, which depends on deal timing. When advising a partner on how to level up, start by mapping their certified-staff gap and building a training plan to close it before the annual review.

Deal Registration, Lead Routing, and MDF

The economic benefits of the program are what make tier progression worth pursuing. Deal registration discounts increase with tier: a Diamond partner registering an opportunity earns a deeper margin than a Silver partner on the same deal. This protects the partner's investment in developing the opportunity and rewards those who bring the most value and volume.

Lead routing priority is a second major benefit. When Mend generates inbound demand in a territory, higher-tier partners receive first right of refusal on qualified leads. Platinum and Diamond partners sit at the front of the routing queue, which compounds their advantage: more leads produce more revenue, which sustains their tier and the priority that comes with it.

Market Development Funds (MDF) are co-investment dollars that Mend provides to fund partner-led marketing — events, campaigns, and demand-generation activities. MDF availability and the co-investment ratio scale with tier, and funds typically require a pre-approved plan and proof of performance. Used well, MDF turns a partner's marketing spend into a shared pipeline-generation engine.

Planning Tier Progression

Tier advancement should be a deliberate, jointly owned plan rather than an accident of good quarters. Start by benchmarking the partner against the next tier's requirements across all three buckets — certifications, revenue, and commitment — and identify the specific gaps. Most partners find that one or two dimensions are lagging while the others are already met, which focuses the plan.

Build a time-bound roadmap toward the annual review. Sequence certification training early because it is controllable and fast. Layer in a pipeline plan that shows how the partner will reach the revenue threshold, using deal registration and MDF-funded demand generation to accelerate. Document the joint business plan that higher tiers require, capturing shared goals, target accounts, and co-marketing commitments.

Finally, treat the annual review as a checkpoint, not a surprise. Partners who track progress quarterly against their advancement plan enter the review with evidence in hand and rarely get down-leveled. The message to every partner is the same: progression is earned through consistent investment in competency and revenue, and Mend invests more in partners who invest more in the relationship.

  1. Benchmark the partner against the next tier's certification, revenue, and commitment requirements.
  2. Close the certification gap first — it is the fastest, most controllable lever.
  3. Build a pipeline plan using deal registration and MDF-funded demand generation to hit the revenue threshold.
  4. Document a joint business plan with shared target accounts and co-marketing commitments.
  5. Review quarterly so the annual evaluation is a confirmation, not a surprise.
