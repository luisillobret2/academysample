Compliance & Regulatory Overview

🕐 30 min 📚 4 sections ⭐ 150 XP Level 1 - Beginner

Why Compliance Matters for AppSec

Regulatory compliance and industry standards increasingly require organizations to demonstrate secure software development practices. Application security is no longer optional — it's a business requirement driven by regulation, customer expectations, and contractual obligations.

Key Compliance Drivers

📜

Regulatory Requirements

GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, and PCI DSS mandate security controls for data protection and privacy.

💰

Customer Requirements

Enterprise customers require SOC 2, ISO 27001, or vendor security questionnaires before purchasing.

🌎

Government Mandates

Executive Order 14028, EU Cyber Resilience Act, and FedRAMP require SBOM and supply chain security.

💰

Insurance Requirements

Cyber insurance providers increasingly require proof of security practices for coverage eligibility.

Common Compliance Frameworks

Understanding the most common frameworks helps you prioritize security controls that satisfy multiple requirements:

SOC 2 Type II

The most common framework for SaaS companies. Covers five Trust Service Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, and Privacy. Requires annual audits by a certified CPA firm.

ISO 27001

International standard for information security management systems (ISMS). Annex A includes controls for secure development (A.14), supplier relationships (A.15), and incident management (A.16).

NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

Organized around five functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover. Widely adopted as a voluntary framework and increasingly referenced in regulations.

SSDF (Secure Software Development Framework)

NIST SP 800-218 defines practices for secure software development. Required for US government software suppliers and increasingly adopted by enterprises.

💡 Getting Started

If your organization is just starting its compliance journey, begin with SOC 2 Type II — it's the most commonly requested framework by enterprise customers and covers the broadest set of security controls.

The SBOM Mandate

Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) requirements are becoming universal:

  • US Executive Order 14028 — Requires SBOMs for all software sold to the US government
  • EU Cyber Resilience Act — Mandates SBOMs and vulnerability handling for products sold in the EU
  • FDA Guidance — Medical device manufacturers must provide SBOMs for cybersecurity documentation
  • NTIA Minimum Elements — Defines the minimum data fields required in an SBOM

Mend generates SBOMs automatically as part of the scanning process, ensuring your organization is always ready for compliance requests.
