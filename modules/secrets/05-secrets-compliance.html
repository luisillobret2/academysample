Regulatory Requirements for Secrets Management

Multiple compliance frameworks mandate proper secrets management practices. Understanding these requirements helps organizations build policies that satisfy auditors while maintaining developer productivity.

💳

PCI DSS 4.0

Requirement 8.6: Prohibits hardcoded passwords. Requires cryptographic key rotation and secure key storage for payment systems.

🏥

SOC 2 Type II

CC6.1: Logical access controls must include credential management, rotation policies, and audit logging.

🎯

HIPAA

164.312(d): Person/entity authentication requires unique credentials with defined lifecycle management.

🌎

GDPR

Article 32: Technical measures for security must include encryption key management and access controls for personal data.

Building Secrets Policies in Mend

Mend enables organizations to define and enforce secrets policies at scale. Policies can be applied at the organization, repository, or branch level:

Policy Configuration

# .mend/secrets-policy.yml
policies:
  - name: "Block High-Severity Secrets"
    severity: high
    action: block
    scope: all-branches

  - name: "Warn on Medium Secrets in Non-Production"
    severity: medium
    action: warn
    scope:
      branches: ["develop", "staging"]

  - name: "Enforce Rotation Age"
    type: rotation-check
    max_age_days: 90
    action: alert
    notify: ["security-team@company.com"]

  - name: "Allowlist Test Fixtures"
    type: allowlist
    paths: ["test/fixtures/**", "**/*.test.*"]
    reason: "Test data with dummy credentials"

Audit & Reporting

Comprehensive audit trails are essential for compliance. Mend provides detailed reporting on secrets findings across your organization:

  • Discovery Reports — Total secrets found, categorized by type, severity, and repository
  • Remediation Tracking — Mean time to remediate (MTTR) for detected secrets, trend analysis
  • Compliance Scorecards — Organization-wide compliance percentage against your defined policies
  • Exposure Timeline — How long each secret was exposed before detection and remediation

💡 Best Practice

Generate monthly secrets compliance reports and share them with your security leadership. Tracking MTTR trends over time demonstrates continuous improvement to auditors.

Organizational Secrets Governance

Effective secrets governance requires coordination across development, security, and operations teams:

  • Secrets Ownership — Every secret must have a designated owner responsible for rotation and decommissioning
  • Onboarding/Offboarding — Automated processes to grant and revoke access when team members join or leave
  • Emergency Procedures — Documented runbooks for mass rotation when a breach is suspected
  • Developer Training — Regular training on secure credential handling and secrets manager usage
  • Exception Management — Formal process for requesting, approving, and time-limiting policy exceptions
