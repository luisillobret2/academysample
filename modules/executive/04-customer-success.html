Driving Customer Success with Mend

For partners, customer success directly drives revenue through renewals, expansions, and referrals. A well-structured customer success program ensures customers realize value from Mend quickly and continue expanding their usage.

The Customer Success Lifecycle

🚀

Onboarding (Days 1-30)

Deploy to initial repositories, configure policies, train core team. Success metric: first scan within 24 hours.

📈

Adoption (Days 31-90)

Expand to more teams, integrate into CI/CD, establish remediation workflows. Success metric: 80% repo coverage.

⭐

Value Realization (Days 91-180)

Demonstrate ROI with metrics, achieve compliance goals, reduce MTTR. Success metric: measurable risk reduction.

💰

Expansion (Day 180+)

Add product modules (SAST, containers, secrets), expand to new business units, upsell services.

Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)

QBRs are the most important touchpoint for demonstrating value and identifying expansion opportunities:

QBR Agenda Template

  1. Executive Summary — Key achievements since last QBR (5 min)
  2. Usage & Adoption Metrics — Scan coverage, active users, policy compliance (10 min)
  3. Security Outcomes — MTTR trends, vulnerability reduction, incidents prevented (10 min)
  4. ROI Review — Quantified value delivered vs. investment (10 min)
  5. Roadmap Preview — Upcoming Mend features relevant to the customer (10 min)
  6. Expansion Opportunities — Areas where Mend can deliver additional value (10 min)
  7. Action Items — Agreed next steps and owners (5 min)

Expansion Playbook

The most effective expansion strategies for Mend customers:

  • Product Expansion — Start with SCA, add SAST, then containers and secrets detection. Each adds incremental coverage.
  • Team Expansion — Start with the security team, expand to platform engineering, then application development teams.
  • Use Case Expansion — Move from vulnerability scanning to SBOM management, compliance automation, and developer enablement.
  • Services Expansion — Offer assessment, implementation, training, and managed security services around the Mend platform.

Churn Prevention

Proactively identify and address churn risks before renewal:

  • Usage Monitoring — Declining scan frequency or active users signals disengagement
  • Champion Changes — When your key advocate changes roles, re-establish relationships immediately
  • Competitor Evaluations — If the customer is evaluating alternatives, engage proactively with an updated value assessment
  • Unresolved Issues — Track and resolve support tickets quickly; unresolved issues are a top churn predictor

💡 Success Tip

The best leading indicator of renewal is executive engagement. If security or engineering leadership attends QBRs and references Mend in their strategy documents, the renewal is secure. If they stop attending, treat it as an urgent churn risk.
