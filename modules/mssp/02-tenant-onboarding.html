Multi-Tenant Architecture & Tenant Isolation

An MSSP’s credibility depends on one absolute guarantee: no customer can ever see another customer’s data. Mend’s multi-tenant architecture enforces this through logical tenant isolation. Every customer is represented by a tenant — a fully segregated logical container that holds its projects, findings, policies, users, and reports. Tenant boundaries are enforced at the data-access layer on every query, so even a bug in a UI component cannot leak data across tenants because the backend will not return rows that do not belong to the caller’s tenant.

Isolation extends beyond data access. Each tenant has its own policy namespace, its own integration configuration (Jira instances, Slack workspaces, CI tokens), and its own audit log. A policy created for Tenant A never appears in Tenant B’s console. An integration token scoped to Tenant A cannot be used to scan Tenant B’s repositories. This strict separation lets an MSSP serve competing customers from the same platform instance without any risk of cross-contamination.

💡 Why Logical Isolation Wins for MSSPs

Some platforms offer physical (per-customer) isolation instead of logical isolation. Physical isolation is simpler to reason about but creates an operational nightmare at scale: hundreds of instances to patch, upgrade, monitor, and back up. Logical isolation gives the MSSP one platform to operate while preserving the data boundary customers demand. Mend’s logical isolation is independently audited and is the foundation of its MSSP suitability.

Customer Onboarding Workflows

Onboarding a new customer tenant is the first operational moment of truth for an MSSP. A smooth onboarding builds confidence; a fumbled one creates doubt that lingers for the life of the contract. Mend supports two onboarding modes: guided onboarding, where the MSSP’s operations team creates and configures the tenant on the customer’s behalf, and self-service provisioning, where the customer (or the MSSP’s sales engineer) steps through a wizard that creates the tenant, connects repositories, and applies a starter policy template.

For MSSPs onboarding many customers in a quarter, bulk onboarding accelerates the process. A provisioning API and CSV import let the operations team create dozens of tenants, assign administrators, and apply policy templates in a single batch. Bulk onboarding is especially valuable when an MSSP wins a large enterprise with many business units, each of which becomes its own tenant with its own administrator but rolls up to a parent account for consolidated reporting.

  1. Create the tenant — Provision a new tenant container, assign a tenant administrator, and apply a starter policy template appropriate to the customer’s industry (e.g., Regulated Workloads for financial services).
  2. Connect source repositories — Link the customer’s GitHub, GitLab, or Bitbucket organizations so Mend can discover and scan repositories. Use scoped tokens with least-privilege access.
  3. Configure integrations — Wire up the customer’s Jira, Slack, or Teams instances so findings route to the systems their developers use. Validate each integration with a test finding.
  4. Apply branding — Customize the tenant’s logo, color scheme, and report footer so the customer sees a tailored experience, whether branded as the MSSP or co-branded.
  5. Run a baseline scan — Execute the first full scan across all connected repositories and review the baseline findings with the customer to set expectations and remediation priorities.

💡 The First-Scan Review

Schedule a baseline-review meeting within one week of onboarding. Walking the customer through their initial findings — especially the Criticals and the reachability-filtered noise reduction — converts a list of vulnerabilities into a shared action plan and establishes the MSSP as a partner rather than a vendor.

Role-Based Access & Data Segregation per Tenant

Within each tenant, Mend’s role-based access control (RBAC) lets the MSSP and the customer share administration safely. The MSSP’s operations engineers hold platform-level roles that span tenants (for support and reporting), while the customer’s administrators hold tenant-scoped roles that grant access only to their own data. This dual-layer model means an MSSP engineer can help a customer configure a policy without ever being able to exfiltrate that customer’s findings to another tenant.

Common tenant-scoped roles include Tenant Administrator (full configuration within the tenant), Security Analyst (view and triage findings, manage policies), Developer (view findings in assigned projects, remediate), and Read-Only Viewer (dashboards and reports only). Roles can be customized per tenant, so a regulated customer can enforce a stricter separation-of-duties matrix than a less-regulated one. Every role assignment is captured in the tenant’s audit log for compliance evidence.

🔒

Data Segregation

Findings, policies, reports, and integrations are all scoped to the tenant. A query from any user returns only the rows that belong to that user’s tenant, enforced at the data-access layer rather than in the UI.

🎨

Branding Customization

Each tenant can carry its own logo, color scheme, and report footer. MSSPs can white-label the platform under their own brand or co-brand with the customer, supporting both reseller and managed-service delivery models.

⚠️ Token Hygiene

Integration tokens scoped to one tenant must never be reused for another. A shared token is the most common cause of accidental cross-tenant access in multi-tenant platforms. Enforce a one-token-per-tenant-per-integration rule and rotate tokens on a regular schedule.
