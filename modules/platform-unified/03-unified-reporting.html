Unified Reporting & Dashboards

🕒 35 min 📚 4 sections ⭐ 175 XP Level 3 - Advanced

In this module you will cover:

  • Build cross-product dashboards that aggregate SCA, SAST, Container, and Secrets metrics
  • Produce executive reporting and security posture metrics for leadership
  • Use the custom report builder and schedule recurring stakeholder-specific reports
  • Export and share reports with auditors, executives, and engineering leaders

Cross-Product Dashboards

The hallmark of a unified platform is a single pane of glass where every security finding — regardless of which product discovered it — appears in one coherent view. Mend’s cross-product dashboards pull data from SCA, SAST, Container security, and Secrets scanning into shared widgets that can be arranged, filtered, and saved as personal or shared views. Instead of logging into four consoles to understand the risk profile of a single application, a security analyst opens one dashboard and sees the full picture.

Every dashboard is built from a common data model. Findings from all products share attributes such as severity, status, project, repository, discovery time, and remediation owner. This shared schema is what makes cross-product aggregation possible: you can build a widget that counts all open Critical findings across every product, or a chart that shows mean time to remediate broken down by finding source. Dashboards refresh on a configurable cadence and can be pinned to wall displays in a security operations center.

📊

Security Posture Score

A single normalized score (0-100) rolls up every product’s findings into one number leadership can track over time. The score weights Critical findings heavily and rewards remediation velocity, giving executives a tangible measure of whether the organization is getting safer.

📅

Trend Analysis

Time-series widgets plot finding counts, remediation times, and policy compliance across weeks, months, or quarters — segmented by product, team, or application. Trends answer the question "are we improving?" far more convincingly than point-in-time snapshots.

Executive Reporting & Stakeholder Views

Different audiences need different reports. A CISO wants a one-page posture summary with trend arrows and a top-five risk list. A VP of Engineering wants mean-time-to-remediate by team and a burndown of the backlog. A compliance officer wants control-by-control evidence tied to a specific framework. Mend addresses this through stakeholder-specific report templates that pre-filter and format the same underlying data for each audience.

Executive reports are designed to be skimmed in under two minutes. They lead with the security posture score and its delta since the last period, followed by a short list of the most significant risks and the actions being taken. Technical detail is relegated to appendices so the narrative stays crisp. Reports can be branded with the organization’s logo and color scheme, and scheduled to land in a stakeholder’s inbox on a daily, weekly, or monthly cadence.

💡 The "One Number" Principle

Executives rarely engage with a wall of metrics. Lead every executive report with a single posture score and its trend. Once leadership trusts that number, the supporting detail becomes reference material rather than the main message.

Custom Report Builder & Scheduled Delivery

When the built-in templates do not fit a specific need, the custom report builder lets you assemble a report from widgets, tables, and narrative blocks. You pick a scope (organization, project, or repository), a time window, and the products to include, then drag widgets onto a canvas. Saved reports can be cloned and adjusted, building a library that matures with the organization’s reporting needs.

  1. Define scope — Choose the projects, repositories, or business units the report covers. Scopes can be static lists or dynamic filters (for example, "all repositories tagged production").
  2. Select widgets — Add posture scores, finding tables, trend charts, top-risk lists, and remediation burndowns. Configure each widget’s filters and grouping.
  3. Schedule delivery — Set a cadence (daily, weekly, monthly), a format (PDF, HTML, CSV), and recipients. Reports can also be triggered by webhooks for event-driven delivery.
  4. Export and share — Generate one-off exports for audits, board meetings, or customer due-diligence requests. Exports retain the access controls of the original report so sensitive data is not leaked.

💡 Access-Aware Sharing

When you share a dashboard or report with a colleague, Mend applies that person’s role-based access to the underlying data. A manager who can see three projects will receive a report covering only those three, even if the report author can see ten. This prevents accidental data overexposure when reports circulate widely.
