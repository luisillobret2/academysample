Migration & Consolidation Strategies

🕒 40 min 📚 4 sections ⭐ 200 XP Level 3 - Advanced

🎯 Learning Objectives

In this module you will cover:

  • How to plan and execute migrations from competing tools such as Snyk, Veracode, Checkmarx, and Black Duck to Mend
  • How to handle data migration, parallel-running, cutover planning, and consolidation of multiple tools into Mend
  • How to retrain teams, manage historical findings, and measure migration success with clear KPIs

Migration Planning Framework

Moving from one or more AppSec tools to Mend is a change-management project as much as a technical one. A successful migration protects the customer's existing security posture while delivering the benefits of consolidation and modernization.

Begin by understanding why the customer is considering migration. Common drivers include license consolidation, reducing tool sprawl, improving developer experience, lowering false positives, and unifying reporting across the enterprise.

Choose parallel-run, phased, or big-bang cutover based on risk and scale.

💡 Migration Tip

Never decommission an existing tool until the new Mend workflow has been validated in production for at least one full sprint. Parallel running reduces rollback risk and gives teams confidence.

Competitor Migration Considerations

Each incumbent tool has different strengths, data formats, and integration patterns. Understanding these differences helps you anticipate migration challenges and position Mend effectively.

📦

From Snyk

SCA and container scanning overlap is high. Map Snyk projects to Mend projects, reconcile .snyk ignore files with Mend policy rules, and align IDE and CI integrations.

💻

From Veracode

SAST and DAST overlap. Expect different scan models; Mend SAST does not require compiled artifacts. Plan to retrain teams on pipeline-based scanning.

🐋

From Checkmarx

SAST and code repositories. Reconcile scan presets and query packs with Mend's default and custom rule sets. Align issue severities to avoid noise.

🔑

From Black Duck

Strong SCA and license overlap. Plan for BOM format conversion, project hierarchy mapping, and license policy reconciliation.

Data Migration Considerations

Historical scan data and findings rarely migrate one-to-one. Instead of trying to replicate every record, focus on what matters:

  • Project inventory: Map existing projects to Mend projects and repositories.
  • Policy rules: Translate existing ignore rules, severity thresholds, and compliance requirements into Mend policies.
  • User access: Provision users and roles in Mend, ideally synced with the customer's identity provider.
  • Integrations: Recreate Jira, Slack, CI/CD, and SIEM connections in the Mend platform.
  • Historical findings: Decide whether to import open findings or accept a fresh baseline. Import is complex; fresh baseline is usually cleaner.

Cutover Strategies

  • Big-bang cutover: Switch all teams at once. Fastest but riskiest; suitable for small, homogeneous environments.
  • Phased cutover: Migrate by business unit, application tier, or technology stack. Reduces risk and allows lessons learned to be applied.
  • Shadow mode: Mend runs in parallel without blocking builds. Teams review results before Mend policies become enforced.
  • Gated cutover: Mend policies become enforcement gates only after a team has tuned thresholds and verified results.

Key Concept: Rollback Plan

Every migration needs a rollback plan. Document how to re-enable the legacy tool, which CI steps to revert, and who is authorized to make the decision. A clear rollback plan reduces stakeholder anxiety and protects the customer if issues arise.

Team Retraining and Measuring Success

Tool migration fails when teams do not know how to use the new workflow. Retraining should be role-specific and timed to the cutover schedule.

  1. Executive Briefing — Explain why the migration is happening and the expected business outcomes.
  2. Security Analyst Training — Deep dive into Mend dashboards, policies, reporting, and vulnerability triage.
  3. Developer Training — IDE plugins, PR workflows, remediation guidance, and how to handle policy violations.
  4. DevOps Training — CI/CD integration, scanner configuration, and pipeline troubleshooting.
  5. Office Hours — Schedule recurring open sessions during the first month after cutover.

Migration Success Metrics

Define metrics before the migration starts and report them at regular checkpoints. Typical KPIs include:

  • Coverage: Percentage of repositories and pipelines scanned by Mend.
  • Scan success rate: Percentage of scans completing without errors.
  • MTTR: Mean time to remediate high-severity findings compared to the legacy tool.
  • False-positive rate: Noise reduction in developer-facing results.
  • Tool consolidation: Number of legacy tools retired and licenses not renewed.
  • Adoption: Active users, IDE plugin installs, and PR comments generated.

At the end of the migration, produce a retrospective report. Include what worked, what was adjusted, final KPIs, and recommendations for ongoing optimization. This document helps secure renewal and expansion conversations.
