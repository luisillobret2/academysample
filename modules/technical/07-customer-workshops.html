Customer Workshop Facilitation

🕒 35 min 📚 4 sections ⭐ 175 XP Level 2 - Intermediate

🎯 Learning Objectives

In this module you will cover:

  • How to design and facilitate customer workshops tailored to executive, engineering, and security audiences
  • How to run interactive exercises, whiteboarding sessions, and gap-analysis workshops that drive alignment
  • How to capture stakeholder buy-in, build consensus, and deliver clear follow-up artifacts after every workshop

Workshop Design Principles

A well-run customer workshop converts technical evaluations into shared commitment. Whether you are presenting to a CISO, a DevOps lead, or a team of developers, the workshop must be audience-first, outcome-driven, and interactive.

Start with the end in mind. Define the single most important decision or alignment you need from the session, then build every agenda item around achieving that outcome.

🎯

Outcome First

Define the desired decision, deliverable, or consensus before designing activities.

👥

Audience Aware

Tailor depth, terminology, and demos to executives, security, or engineering.

🚀

Interactive

Limit slides. Use whiteboards, polls, and hands-on exercises to maintain engagement.

📝

Action Oriented

End every session with owners, next steps, and a written summary.

💡 Facilitation Tip

Send a pre-read one business day before the workshop. Include the agenda, expected outcomes, and any links or credentials needed. This reduces setup time and increases participation.

Agenda Templates by Audience

Different stakeholders care about different outcomes. Use these templates as a starting point and customize them based on the customer's maturity, compliance drivers, and current tooling.

Executive Workshop (60 minutes)

📈

Business Context

Industry trends, supply-chain risk, compliance pressures, and cost of AppSec debt.

👥

Current State

High-level summary of tools, coverage, and gaps identified during discovery.

💡

Mend Value Proposition

Risk reduction, consolidation, developer experience, and measurable ROI.

👥

Decision & Next Steps

Scope, timeline, success criteria, and executive sponsorship.

Engineering Workshop (90 minutes)

Engineering audiences want to see the developer workflow. Include live demos, IDE integrations, PR workflows, and CI/CD pipeline behavior. Leave plenty of time for questions about scan configuration, false positives, and remediation.

Security Workshop (90 minutes)

Security audiences want policy, reporting, and governance. Cover policy engines, severity models, compliance dashboards, SBOM generation, integration with SIEM/SOAR, and vulnerability prioritization. Be prepared to discuss Mend's data handling and access controls.

Interactive Exercises and Whiteboarding

Interactive exercises transform passive listeners into active participants. They also surface real concerns and constraints that might not appear in a slide deck.

  1. Current-State Mapping — Draw the customer's existing AppSec pipeline on a whiteboard. Identify scan points, handoffs, and data flows.
  2. Gap Analysis Voting — Give each participant three sticky notes to vote on the biggest AppSec pain points. Discuss the winners as a group.
  3. Future-State Whiteboard — Collaboratively sketch how Mend integrates into the pipeline. Ask the customer to place components and draw connection lines.
  4. Prioritization Matrix — Plot remediation actions by impact and effort. Agree on quick wins and long-term initiatives.

🎥 Example: Whiteboarding a Mend CI/CD integration with a customer's DevOps team.

Facilitation Best Practices

  • Ask before telling. Use open questions like "Where do most vulnerabilities slip through today?" to surface real pain points.
  • Manage the loud voices. Actively invite quieter participants to contribute so security, engineering, and compliance all feel heard.
  • Stay neutral. Avoid disparaging the customer's current tools. Position Mend as a complement or consolidation path, not a criticism.
  • Capture visibly. Take notes on a shared screen or document so participants see their input being recorded.

Follow-Up and Deliverables

The value of a workshop is often realized in the follow-up. Send a summary within 24 hours while the discussion is fresh. A good follow-up package reinforces commitment and makes the next step obvious.

📋

Workshop Summary

Participants, agenda, key decisions, and agreed-upon next steps with owners.

📈

Gap Analysis

Current-state coverage, identified gaps, and Mend capabilities mapped to each.

👥

Roadmap

Phased deployment plan, pilot scope, success metrics, and timeline.

📝

Action Items

Specific tasks, owners, and due dates for both Mend and customer teams.

Schedule the next meeting before the workshop ends. If possible, get a commitment to the proof-of-concept scope or a follow-up architecture review. Momentum matters more than perfect documentation.
