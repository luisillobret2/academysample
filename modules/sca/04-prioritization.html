Prioritize Threats Based on Severity

With thousands of CVEs published annually, knowing what to fix first is as important as finding vulnerabilities. Mend SCA leverages comprehensive vulnerability assessment to identify true risks affecting your application, including those powered by AI.

Mend’s Risk Prioritization Stack

Mend combines four signals to create an effective prioritization:

📈

CVSS 4.0 Severity

The Common Vulnerability Scoring System measures the intrinsic qualities of a vulnerability. Mend uses the latest CVSS 4.0 standard for more accurate severity classification.

🎯

EPSS Prediction

The Exploit Prediction Scoring System estimates the probability a CVE will be exploited in the wild within 30 days, ranging from 0% to 100%.

🔗

Reachability Status

Whether your application code actually calls the vulnerable function. Unreachable CVEs are deprioritized regardless of severity.

🔧

Fix Availability

Whether a patched version of the dependency exists. Vulnerabilities with available fixes are prioritized since remediation is actionable.

Understanding EPSS

EPSS is a data-driven effort to estimate the likelihood that a software vulnerability will be exploited in the wild. It produces a score between 0 and 1 (0% to 100%).

💡 Why EPSS Matters More Than CVSS Alone

CVSS measures theoretical severity — how bad a vulnerability could be if exploited. EPSS measures practical likelihood — how likely it is to actually be exploited. A CVSS 9.8 Critical vulnerability with an EPSS of 0.01% is far less urgent than a CVSS 7.5 High vulnerability with an EPSS of 85%. Mend combines both to surface the most actionable risks.
